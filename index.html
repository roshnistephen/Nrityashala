The Art Form

The Grace of Odissi

✦

Odissi is one of the oldest surviving dance forms of India, originating from the sacred temples of Odisha. Distinguished by its lyrical grace, sensuous movements, and deeply spiritual expression, Odissi is built upon the tribhangi — the three-point deflection of the body representing the divine beauty of Lord Jagannath.

Every gesture, every glance, every footstep in Odissi is a prayer — a conversation between the dancer and the divine. It is an art that transcends performance and becomes a meditative offering.

🌸

Abhinaya

The art of expressive storytelling through facial expressions, gestures, and emotion — bhava, raga, tala.

🌿

Nritta

Pure rhythmic dance — the body as an instrument of music, moving in perfect harmony with the beats.

🪷

Nritya

The blending of pure dance and expression — where technique meets devotion in exquisite harmony.

About the Guru

Smt. Preeti Mohapatra

Disciple of Two Revered Gurus

✦

Mrs. Preeti Mohapatra, an accomplished Odissi exponent, stands among the disciples of the legendary Padma Vibhushan Guru Sri Kelucharan Mohapatra and Guru Sri Naba Kishore Mishra. A recipient of the National Scholarship for both Odissi and Chhau, she embodies the true essence of India's classical dance heritage.

Over a 40-year artistic journey, including 25 years as a dedicated Odissi guru, she has carved a niche as both a performer of exceptional grace and a teacher of rare depth. A graded artist of Doordarshan, Bhubaneswar, she has performed at prestigious stages across India.
